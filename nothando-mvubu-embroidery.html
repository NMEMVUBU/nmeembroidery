About

A craft passed
through generations.

I started embroidery at the age of ten. My mother worked as a domestic helper for a family with a deep love of arts and crafts, and it was there that I first encountered a needle and thread. But the real roots run deeper — my grandmother, who was also my mother's fashion teacher in school, had passed down everything she knew about needlework, and my mother carried that knowledge with her. I inherited it.

I began with cross stitch, learning the patience and precision the craft demands, and I have never stopped. Since then, I have created a piece exhibited at FEDE Art House in Cape Town, and my work has inspired a brand collection. I am currently focused on original embroidery pieces — each one a way of saying what I cannot say in any other form. Embroidery is vast. It is accessible. And it is made entirely by hand, always.
